## Today's Digest

### Day 6: Real-World Applications and Adjacent Fields in the US-China Rivalry

The US-China rivalry, emblematic of the broader Great Power Competition, permeates numerous sectors and disciplines, shaping everything from global trade to technological innovation. This day’s exploration will delve into concrete examples where this rivalry manifests, illustrating its profound implications across various fields. Unexpectedly, we’ll also uncover its influence in cultural spheres, such as sports and entertainment, revealing how power dynamics can extend far beyond traditional geopolitical engagements.

#### 1. Technology and Cybersecurity: The Race for Dominance

One of the clearest arenas of competition is technology, particularly in the realms of artificial intelligence (AI), 5G telecommunications, and cybersecurity. The US has taken significant steps to counter China's ascendance in these fields. For instance, the US government has imposed restrictions on Chinese tech giants like Huawei and ZTE, citing national security concerns. These moves not only limit Chinese access to advanced chip technology but also encourage domestic companies to innovate and produce alternatives, such as the US-based company Qualcomm, which has stepped up efforts to lead in 5G technology.

Moreover, the cybersecurity landscape is a battleground where the US and China engage in espionage and counter-espionage. According to a report by the cybersecurity firm Mandiant, Chinese state-sponsored hackers have been implicated in numerous attacks on US corporations, aiming to steal trade secrets and intellectual property. This has led to increased investment in cybersecurity solutions, with US firms like CrowdStrike and FireEye seeing significant growth in demand for their services. 

#### 2. Trade and Economic Policy: Tariffs and Supply Chains

The economic dimension of the rivalry is equally pronounced, most notably through tariffs and the reshaping of global supply chains. The trade war initiated by the Trump administration in 2018 saw the imposition of tariffs on hundreds of billions of dollars’ worth of Chinese goods, provoking retaliatory tariffs from China. This tit-for-tat has forced companies to rethink their supply chains, with many US manufacturers relocating to countries like Vietnam or India to avoid tariffs while still accessing Asian markets. 

For example, Apple has been diversifying its supply chain, investing in production facilities in India and expanding its manufacturing footprint in other Southeast Asian countries. This strategic pivot not only mitigates risk but also aligns with the broader US objective of reducing dependence on China.

#### 3. Military and Defense: The Indo-Pacific Strategy

In the military domain, the US-China rivalry has compelled a reevaluation of defense strategies, particularly in the Indo-Pacific region. The US has strengthened alliances with countries like Japan, Australia, and India through initiatives such as the Quad, aimed at countering Chinese influence. China's military expansion, particularly in the South China Sea, has prompted the US to increase its naval presence in the region, conducting freedom of navigation operations to assert its commitment to international maritime law.

A striking example of this military posturing is the US's partnership with the Philippines, which has been revitalized with a focus on joint military exercises and infrastructure development. The Enhanced Defense Cooperation Agreement (EDCA) allows US forces to access Philippine bases, a strategic move countering China's assertiveness.

#### 4. Climate Change and Green Technology: A Surprising Field of Rivalry

Interestingly, the competition extends into the realm of climate change and green technology, where both nations vie for leadership in renewable energy and sustainable practices. The US has made substantial investments in green technologies, including solar and wind energy, spurred by bipartisan recognition of climate change as a critical issue. Companies like Tesla, which has expanded its Gigafactory in Shanghai, symbolize the intersection of climate ambition and economic competition.

Conversely, China has emerged as a leader in solar panel manufacturing and electric vehicle production. The Chinese government has heavily subsidized these industries, aiming for dominance in the global renewable energy market. This competition is not just about economic supremacy but also about soft power, as countries increasingly look to align with leaders in sustainable practices.

#### 5. Cultural Influence: Sports and Entertainment as a Battleground

Perhaps one of the most unexpected arenas where the US-China rivalry plays out is in sports and entertainment. The NBA, for instance, found itself at the center of a diplomatic storm when Houston Rockets GM Daryl Morey tweeted support for pro-democracy protests in Hong Kong. The backlash from China was swift, leading to a significant loss of revenue for the league and highlighting how cultural platforms can become battlegrounds for geopolitical disputes.

In the realm of film and television, the Chinese market has become crucial for Hollywood studios. Films like “Fast & Furious 9” strategically include Chinese elements or stars to appeal to the lucrative market, reflecting a desire to navigate the delicate balance between artistic expression and market forces. However, this reliance also creates tension, as studios must self-censor to avoid offending the Chinese government, showcasing how cultural production is intricately linked with the dynamics of power.

### Conclusion

The US-China rivalry is a multifaceted phenomenon that extends far beyond military and economic competition. Its implications ripple through technology, trade, military alliances, and even cultural spheres. By examining these concrete examples, we can better understand how the rivalry shapes our world and influences the trajectory of global power dynamics. As we prepare for tomorrow's final day of exploration, we will reflect on the future of this rivalry and its potential resolutions or escalations in the coming years.

## Reference Pick

**'Destined for War' — Graham Allison**

Graham Allison's 'Destined for War' is an essential read for anyone delving into the dynamics of great power competition, particularly as it pertains to the U.S.-China rivalry. Allison articulates the Thucydides Trap—a concept that posits that when a rising power challenges an established one, conflict is often inevitable. This book goes beyond theoretical frameworks, providing real-world applications and case studies that illustrate how historical precedents inform contemporary geopolitical tensions. For Day 6/7 of your exploration, Allison's blend of accessible narrative and rigorous analysis offers profound insights into the strategic calculations of both powers, making it a perfect companion as you contextualize the broader implications of competition on global stability.
